Module 2
🔬 Characteristics Bahasa Manusia
Human language is no ordinary communication system. There are 7 unique characteristics that make it extraordinary!
01
🎲
Arbitrary
There is no natural connection between a word's sound and its meaning. "Dog" in Indonesian is "anjing" — why are they different?
02
♾️
Productive
From a finite set of words, you can create unlimited sentences. You can say something that has never been said before!
03
🔁
Duality
Language has two layers: sounds (meaningless alone) & words (meaningful). "b", "a", "t" → "bat"! This is duality of patterning.
04
🚀
Displacement
You can talk about things far away — the past, the future, distant places, or even things that don't exist!
05
🎭
Cultural Transmission
Language is transmitted culturally, not biologically. An Indonesian child raised in Japan will speak Japanese!
06
🎵
Vocal-Auditory
By default, language uses the vocal-auditory channel. But sign language proves that modality can be different!
07
🔄
Interchangeability
Language speakers can act as both speaker and listener — unlike many animal communication systems!
Module 3
🌿 Branches of Linguistics
Linguistics is not a single science — it has many branches, each focusing on a different aspect of language!
🔊
Fonologi
Studies the sound system in language — how sounds function and interact with each other.
Example: /p/ vs /b/ in "pin" and "bin" = different sounds, different meanings!
🎙️
Fonetik
Studies language sounds physically — production, acoustic transmission, and perception by the ear.
Example: The vocal cords vibrate to produce voiced sounds
🧩
Morfologi
Studies the internal structure of words — how morphemes (smallest meaningful units) form words.
Example: "un-write-able" = un- + write + -able → 3 morphemes!
🏗️
Sintaksis
Studies the rules for combining words into grammatical phrases, clauses, and sentences.
Example: "The cat eats fish" ✓ vs "Eats cat fish" — which sounds more natural?
💭
Semantik
Studies meaning in language — the meaning of words, phrases, sentences, and relationships between meanings.
Example: "thin" and "slim" both mean thin, but their contexts differ!
💬
Pragmatik
Studies how context influences meaning — what is meant vs what is said.
Example: "Can you open the window?" = a request, not a question about ability!
👥
Sosiolinguistik
Studies the relationship between language and society — language variation, diglossia, social identity.
Example: Javanese has levels — ngoko, madya, krama!
🧠
Psikolinguistik
Studies the relationship between language and human cognitive/psychological processes — how we process language.
Example: Aphasia (brain damage) can selectively impair specific language abilities!
📜
Linguistik Historis
Studies language change over time and the reconstruction of proto-languages (language ancestors).
Example: Indonesian, Malay, Tagalog — all descended from Proto-Austronesian!
🔬
Neurolinguistik
Studies how language is represented and processed in the human brain.
Example: Broca's & Wernicke's Areas — two language centers in our brain!
